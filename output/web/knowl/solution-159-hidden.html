As above, the number of possible outcomes of the lottery drawing is \(_{48}C_{6}=12,271,512\text{.}\) In order to win the second prize, five of the six numbers on the ticket must match five of the six winning numbers; in other words, we must have chosen five of the six winning numbers and one of the 42 losing numbers. The number of ways to choose 5 out of the 6 winning numbers is given by \(_{6}C_{5}=6\) and the number of ways to choose 1 out of the 42 losing numbers is given by \(_{42}C_{1}=42\text{.}\) Thus the number of favorable outcomes is then given by the Basic Counting Rule: \(_{6}C_{5}\cdot _{42}C_{1}=6\cdot 42=252\text{.}\)
So the probability of winning the second prize is \(\frac{252}{12271512} \approx 0.0000205\)
