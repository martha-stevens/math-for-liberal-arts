Solution 5.2.3.1.

In this example,
\begin{align*} d \amp= \$100\\ r \amp= 0.06\\ k \amp= 12\\ N \amp = 20 \end{align*}
Putting this into the equation:
\begin{equation*} P_N =\frac{100\left( \left(1+\frac{0.06}{12}\right)^{20\cdot 12} - 1\right)}{\left(\frac{.06}{12}\right)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P_N =\frac{100 \left( \left(1.005\right)^{240}-1\right)}{0.005} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P_N =\frac{100\left(3.310 - 1\right)}{0.005} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P_N =\frac{100\left(2.210\right)}{0.005}=\$46200 \end{equation*}
The account will grow to $46,200 after 20 years.
Notice that you deposited into the account a total of $24,000 ($100 a month for 240 months). The difference between what you end up with and how much you put in is the interest earned. In this case it is $46,200 - $24,000 = $22,200.
