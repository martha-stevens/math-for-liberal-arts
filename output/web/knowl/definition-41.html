Definition 5.3.1. Payout Annuity Formula.

To compute the balance at the beginning of a payout annuity with regular withdrawals of d,
\begin{equation*} P_0=\frac{d \left(1 - \left(1+\frac{r}{k}\right)^{-Nk}\right)}{\left(\frac{r}{k}\right)} \end{equation*}
\(P_0\) is the balance in the account at the beginning (starting amount, or principal).
\(d\) is the regular withdrawal (the amount you take out each year, each month, etc.)
\(r\) is the annual interest rate (in decimal form. Example: 5% = 0.05)
\(k\) is the number of compounding periods in one year.
\(N\) is the number of years we plan to take withdrawals
