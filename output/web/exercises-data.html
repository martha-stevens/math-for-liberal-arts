    1.

    Scores on a Math test are listed below.
    80, 90, 50, 100, 50, 80, 90, 70, 70, 30, 70, 80, 100, 80, 60, 70, 70, 100, 80, 60, 70, 60, 50, 50
    1. Complete the frequency table for the Math test scores
    2. Construct a bar graph of the data
    3. Construct a pie chart of the data

    2.

    A group of adults where asked how many cars they had in their household. Their results are listed below.
    1, 1, 4, 2, 2, 1, 2, 3, 1, 2, 2, 2, 3, 1, 3, 2, 1, 1, 4, 1, 2, 1, 2, 2
    1. Complete the frequency table for the car number data
    2. Construct a bar graph of the data
    3. Construct a pie chart of the data

    3.

    A group of adults were asked how many children they have in their families. The bar graph shows the number of adults who indicated each number of children.
    1. How many adults were questioned?
    2. What percentage of the adults questioned had 0 children?
    Answer.
    1. 15
    2. 33%

    7.

    The data below gives the price (in dollars) of a gallon of gasoline at several gas stations in the area.
    2.54, 2.78, 3.23, 3.56, 2.98, 2.77, 3.15, 3.24, 3.35, 2.87, 2.49, 2.63, 3.15
    1. Group the data into class intervals of width 50 cents, starting at $2.00.
    2. Create a histogram of the data.

    8.

    A rec center counted the number of people using the center once every hour on Sundays to decide if it should reduce its hours. The results they obtained are summarized in the graph below.
    1. What was the smallest number of visitors in the rec center and how many times did it occur?
    2. For how many hours were visitors counted?
    3. For what percent of the hours counted were there fewer than 5 visitors in the rec center?
    4. If you were deciding whether to reduce hours on Sundays would you want any additional information?

    For the dataset: {2, 5, 8, 10, 15, 25, 25}
    1. Find the mean
    2. Find the median
    3. Find the mode
    4. Find the first quartile
    5. Find the third quartile
    Answer.
    1. 12.86
    2. 10
    3. 25
    4. 5
    5. 25

    A group of diners were asked how much they would pay for a meal. Their responses were:
    $7.50, $8.25, $9.00, $8.00, $7.25, $7.50, $8.00, $7.00
    1. Find the mean
    2. Find the median

    2. $7.75
    3. $7.00, $7.375, $7.75, $8.125, $9.00
    4. 0.637

    20.

    A manager of a human resources department is investigating the number of sick days employees took during the last year. For employees in marketing, the average number of sick days was 4.5 with a standard deviation of 2. For those in accounting the mean number of sick days was 5.8 with a standard deviation of 3. Compute and compare the coefficients of variation for each set of data.

    21.

    Ellen’s average commute time is 30 minutes with a standard deviation of 8 minutes. Zac’s commute takes an average of 45 minutes with a standard deviation of 10 minutes. Whose commute has more consistent times?
    Hint.
    Compare the coefficients of variation.
    Answer.
    Zac's commute times are more consistent. (The coefficent of variation is 0.22 for Zac and 0.27 for Ellen.)
