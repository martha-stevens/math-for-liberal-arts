Suppose you roll the first die. The probability the second will be different is \(\frac{5}{6}\text{.}\) The probability that the third roll is different than the previous two is \(\frac{4}{6}\text{,}\) so the probability that the three dice are different is \(\frac{5}{6}\cdot \frac{4}{6}=\frac{20}{36}\text{.}\) The probability that two dice will match is the complement, \(1-\frac{20}{36}=\frac{16}{36}\text{.}\)
The expected value is: \(\$2 \cdot \frac{16}{36}+\left(-\$1 \right) \cdot \frac{20}{36} =\frac{12}{36} \approx \$0.33\text{.}\)
