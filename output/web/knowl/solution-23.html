Solution 1.4.3.1.

While you might have a sheet of paper handy, trying to measure it would be tricky. Instead we might imagine a stack of paper, and then scale the thickness and weight to a single sheet. If youâ€™ve ever bought paper for a printer or copier, you probably bought a ream, which contains 500 sheets. We could estimate that a ream of paper is about 2 inches thick and weighs about 5 pounds. Scaling these down,
\(\frac{2\text{ inches}}{\text{ream}} \cdot \frac{1 \text{ ream}}{500 \text{ pages}} = 0.004\) inches per sheet
\(\frac{5\text{ pounds}}{\text{ream}} \cdot \frac{1 \text{ ream}}{500 \text{ pages}} = 0.01\) pounds per sheet, or 0.16 ounces per sheet.
