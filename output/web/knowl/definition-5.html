Definition 1.3.2. Areas of Basic Shapes.

The formulas for both area and distance around (perimeter/circumference) of rectangles and circles are below.
A rectangle with length, \(L\) and width, \(W\) has an area and perimeter of:
\begin{equation*} A = LW \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} P = 2L + 2W \end{equation*}
A circle with radius, \(r\) has an area and circumference of:
\begin{equation*} A = \pi r^2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} C = 2\pi r \end{equation*}
