Item b.

There are 10 possible numbers (0 to 9) that could be in the first digit of the keycode. The second digit has to be different from the first, so that leave 9 possible numbers for the second digit. The third digit must be different from the first two digits, so there are 8 possible numbers for the thrid digit. Using the same reasoning, that leave 7 possibilities for the fourth digit, and:
\begin{equation*} 10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 = 5,040 \end{equation*}
There are 5,040 keycodes that have no repeated digits.
