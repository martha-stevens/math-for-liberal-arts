In 2000, the apportionment is:
  • Jefferson = 8
  • Clay = 4
  • Madison = 10
  • Jackson = 11
  • Franklin = 17
In 2010, the apportionment is:
  • Jefferson = 8
  • Clay = 5
  • Madison = 10
  • Jackson = 11
  • Franklin = 16
This illustrates the population paradox--Franklin's population increased while Clay's stayed constant. Yet, Franklin lost a seat and Clay gained a seat.
