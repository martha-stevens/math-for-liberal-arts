The number of candies in machine-filled boxes are normally distributed with a mean of 30 candies and a standard deviation of 1 candy. Use the 68-95-99.7 rule to determine:
  1. What is the probability that a box of candies will contain more than 32 candies?
  2. What percent of boxes would you expect to contain more than 27 candies?
  3. What percent of boxes would you expect to contain between 32 and 33 candies?
  4. In a shipment of 2,000 boxes, how many would you expect to have less than 28 candies?
