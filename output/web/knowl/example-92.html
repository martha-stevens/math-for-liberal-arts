Example 4.1.13.

Suppose a four year old boy is currently 39 inches tall, and you are told to expect him to grow 2.5 inches a year.
We can set up a growth model, with \(n = 0\) corresponding to 4 years old.
Recursive form:
\begin{align*} P_0 \amp = 39\\ P_n \amp = P_{n-1} + 2.5 \end{align*}
Explicit form:
\begin{equation*} P_n = 39 + 2.5n \end{equation*}
So at 6 years old, we would expect him to be \(P_2 = 39 + 2.5(2) = 44\) inches tall.
Any mathematical model will break down eventually. Certainly, we shouldnâ€™t expect this boy to continue to grow at the same rate all his life. If he did, at age 50 he would be \(P_{46} = 39 + 2.5(46) = 154\) inches tall = 12.8 feet tall!
